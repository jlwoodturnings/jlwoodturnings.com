Slabs and Turning Blanks

All of my material for furniture and turning blanks is sourced from locally harvested storm damaged trees or urban logging. Once milled and dried which can take anywhere from 6 to 12 months, I look to see what I can make out of the material. This results in more organic pieces that tell a story which is one of the best parts of making heirloom pieces of furniture or bowls.

Wood Turning

Wood turning is my favorite form of woodworking. From using a chainsaw to cut the blanks, to putting the final finish on the project, there's nothing more rewarding than watching someone fall in love with the piece when they see it for the first time.

Live Edge Furniture

For me making furniture from live edge slabs is the ultimate way to show off the wood in its natural form. Being able to tell the story of me finding the tree, milling a slab, air drying the slab over the course of a year and making something that compliments the wood is why I build furniture.
